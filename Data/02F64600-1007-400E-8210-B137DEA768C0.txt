Effects on electricity production with adding microalgae in fixed-film 
and suspended-growth microbial fuel cells 
 
Chun-Sheng Wu*, Yen-Chan Lin, Wen-Hsiang Lin, Jau-Hang Du 
*Professor, Department of Leisure Resources and Green Industries, Leader University, 
Tainan City, Taiwan 70970, ROC. E-mail: wujoe38@mail.leader.edu.tw   
 
 
Abstract 
 
Four kinds of microbial fuel cell (MFC) systems, fixed-film MFC (FMFC), 
suspended-growth MFC (SMFC), photosynthetic fixed-film MFC (PFMFC), and 
photosynthetic suspended-growth MFC (PSMFC) were conducted in this study. In the 
photosynthetic MFC (PMFC) systems, including the PFMFC system and the PSMFC 
system, the microalgae Nannochloropsis oculata was added in the cathode chambers. 
The PMFC systems explored in this study were based on the advantages of combining 
the anaerobic digestion system and the microalgae photosynthesis together. The 
PMFC system possesses the following features: (1) The anaerobic digestion system in 
the anode chamber has the capability of pollution removal; (2) The microalgae 
photosynthesis can fix the greenhouse gas CO2 and thus the global warming problem 
will be relieved. During the photosynthesis, the oxygen used for reducing H+ ions is 
produced, and thus the energy for aeration can be saved; (3) The electricity produced 
in the PMFC system can be harvested; (4) The grown microalgae can be used for 
producing biodiesel or feeding animals.  
Glucose was used as the feeding substrate. The results showed that the maximum 
power density (PDmax) 159.7 mW/m2 of the PFMFC system was higher than that 42.7 
mW/m2 of the FMFC system. The coulombic efficiency (CE) of the former system 
was about four times of the latter system. Likewise, the PDmax of the PSMFC system, 
66.0 mW/m2, was also higher than that of the SMFC system, 49.2 mW/m2. The CE 
value of the PSMFC system was close to 1.5 times of that of the SMFC system. These 
results indicate that adding the microalgae can enhance the electricity production 
efficiency of the MFC system, especially, the PFMFC being superior to the PSMFC. 
Moreover, the conventional aeration method to provide oxygen in the cathode 
chamber can be replaced by the photosynthesis of microalgae, thus the power for 
aeration can be saved. 
 
 
Keywords: Microbial fuel cell, photosynthesis, anaerobic digestion, electricity 
production, microalgae

3. Material and methods 
3.1 Establishing and starting up the four MFC systems 
Four MFC (FMFC, SMFC, PFMFC, PSMFC) systems were established (Fig. 1). 
The effective volume of the anode chamber and the cathode chamber was 120 ml (20 
cm (L) " 4 cm (W) " 1.5 cm (H)) each. The operation conditions of four test runs 
were shown in Table 1. In the FMFC system, the reticulated vitreous carbon (RVC) 
was used as the biofilm support media in the anode chamber, and a buffer solution 
was used in the cathode chamber. The buffer solution contains 50 mM NaCl, 100 mM 
NaH2PO4ï¼H2O, and pH of 7.0 adjusted with NaOH. In the SMFC system, an 
anaerobic suspended-growth system was adopted in the anode chamber, and a buffer 
solution was used in the cathode chamber. In the PFMFC system and the PSMFC 
system, the microalgae Nannochloropsis oculata was extra respectively added in the 
cathode chamber of the FMFC system and the SMFC system.  
In the FMFC and the PFMFC systems, we used 88.2 cm3 RVC (i.e., 5 pores per 
inch (PPI), specific surface area 25 m2/m3) as the electricity-conducted material in 
each anode chamber. In the anode chambers of the SMFC system and the PSMFC 
system, 18 cm2 of carbon paper (CP) was used instead. In the cathode chamber of the 
four MFC systems, a volume of 49 cm3 RVC was used as the electricity-conducted 
material. Glucose solution was introduced as the feeding substrate of the anode 
chambers in the four MFC systems. The ingredients in the glucose solution per liter 
was: C6H12O6 2 g, NaHCO3 2.5 g, KCl 0.1g, NaCl 0.1 g, CaCl2ï¼2H2O 0.1 g, NH4Cl 
1.5 g, NaH2PO4 0.6 g, MgSO4ï¼7H2O 0.1 g, MgCl2ï¼6H2O 0.1 g, MnCl2ï¼4H2O 0.005 
g, NaMoO4ï¼ 2H2O 0.001 g, pH of 7.0 adjusted with NaH2PO4ï¼ H2O. A 
semi-penetration membrane, proton exchange membrane (PEM; DuPontTM NafionÂ® 
117, surface area of 80 cm2), was installed between the anode chamber and the 
cathode chamber.

chamber continuously with glucose solution 2000 mg/l, inflow rate (Qi) of 10 ml/h,

4. Results and discussion 
4.1 Effects on PDmax, CE, CD, and Voc with adding microalgae  
Compared the data of Run 3 with that of Run 1 (Table 2), we found the PDmax 
increased from 42.7 mW/m2 (Run 1), to 159.7 mW/m2 (Run 3), and the value of CE 
also increased from 0.59% (Run 1) to 2.35% (Run 3) (Fig. 7). In other words, the 
values of PDmax and CE of the PFMFC system were about 4 times of those of the 
FMFC system. This means that the electricity generation efficiency can be 
significantly enhanced by adding the microalgae in the FMFC system. Moreover, the 
values of CD of the photosynthetic MFC (PFMFC and PSMFC) systems were also 
higher than that of non-photosynthetic MFC (FMFC and SMFC) systems (Fig. 8). The 
PDmax of the PSMFC system, 66.0 mW/m2, was higher than that of the SMFC system. 
The CE of the former, 1.13%, was also higher than that of the latter, 0.72% (Table 2). 
In other words, the electricity generation efficiency can be enhanced by adding the 
microalgae in the SMFC system, as well. 
The PDmax of the PFMFC system, 159.7 mW/m2, was found to be superior to that 
of the PSMFC system, 66.0 mW/m2. The reason speculated was that the biomass 
quantity in the PFMFC system was more than that in the PSMFC system, and a 
shorter electron transmission distance within the biofilm than that within a 
suspended-growth solution. Thus, a higher substrate conversion rate was given, and 
resulted in a higher electron transmission rate (i.e., electricity production rate) from 
the anode chamber to the cathode chamber via the external circuit. 
It was worth to be noted that the RL in the FMFC was 510 â„¦, while in the 
PFMFC was 120 â„¦. Similarly, the RL also shifted from 390 â„¦ of the SMFC system to 
240 â„¦ of the PSMFC system. This means the RL corresponding to the PDmax 
decreased as the microalgae introduced into the MFC system and this will be 
beneficial to the electricity generation.
