moderate softening temperature (670OC) (Ts) and ideal thermal expansion coefficient 
(11.89 ppmK
-1
) (CTE) [6, 7]. The thermal expansion coefficient of G6 glass is near to 
8YSZ, 8 mol% yttria-stabilized zirconia, 20SDC, 20 mol% Sm-doped CeO2 and 
metals [6, 7]. Therefore, metal and alloy can be coated or printed coherently on G6 
glass without significant bending in the condition of high temperature.  
The producing method of G6 multilayer structures is similar to two procedures, 
tape casting and low temperature co-fired ceramics (LTCC) [8]. Chang and Chen had 
success in producing G6 single tape using tape-casting [6, 7]. To further investigate 
the densification, microstructure, and residual carbon content of the substrate, the 
multi-layered glass tapes have been sintered in selected temperature and heating rates 
using LTCC in the study. The purpose of the study is not only trying to obtain dense 
G6 multi-layered glass as excellent substrates for sputter-coating and ink-jet printing, 
but also the power consumption and resistance of the meandering pattern of 
micro-hotplates and sensors can be investigated at different temperature [9-11]  
 
Experimental procedures  
The single layer of G6 tape as the material of substrates in the experiment was 
produced by Exojet Techonology Corporation, Taiwan. The thickness of G6 single 
layer is approximately 100 ğœ‡m. The G6 multilayer (3x3 cm2) was fabricated by 
thermal compression machine (VC-10 tons, Lin Cheng Machinery Co., Taiwan) with 
loading pressure of 100 Kgcm
-2
 and holding at 140
o
C for 30 min. After thermal 
compressing, the G6 multilayers were sintered following the different thermal cycle 
so to investigate the densification, microstructure, and residual carbon content of the 
thin substrate (Table 1). The G6 multilayers were heated to 670
o
C and 680
o
C at the 
rates of 2, 4, 8 and 10
o
C/min and holding at 670
o
C and 680
o
C for 1, 2 and 4 h, 
followed by cooling to room temperature at a rate of 10
o
C/min. The fracture surfaces 
of sintered G6 multilayers were observed using scanning electron microscopy (JEOL 
JSM6510) with magnification of 3000X (Fig. 2). The volume density of sintered G6 
multilayer was determined upon hydrostatics and Archimedes' Principle [12], and 
compared to the theoretical volume density of 3.72 g/cm
3 
(Table 1). The residual 
carbon content of sintered G6 multilayers was measured using EMIA-V2 series 
Carbon/Sulfur Combustion Analyzer.  
     After the preparation of G6 multilayers as substrates for the usage of sensors 
and micro-hotplates, sensors and micro-hotplates were made by Pt thin film, Ni ink 
and Fe alloy ink, which were coated and printed on multi-layer glass-ceramic 
structure for usage of solid oxide fuel cell (SOFC). The sintered G6 multilayers were 
grinding and polishing before sputter-coating and ink-jet printing. The sensors and 
micro-hotplates made of Pt thin film were fabricated using sputter-coating (108 Auto 
Sputter Coater) with the operational current of 40mA for 120 s, 150 s and 180 s (Fig. 
2). The SUS304 shadow mask was fabricated by Micro-Electro-Mechanical Systems 
(MEMS), STEKEN CO., LTD. The two meandering patterns of masks were designed 
with a dimension of line width of 300 Î¼m and thickness of 0.1 mm for Pt 
sputter-coating. (Fig. 3) The demanded thickness (100 nm) of Pt thin film was

    Fig. 4 plots the thickness of sputter-coating Pt pattern analysis results. The

Conclusion 
G6 multilayers become denser with lesser binder residuals and voids with 
increasing the holding time and temperature. The ideal G6 multilayer was sintered at a 
rate of 10
o
Cmin
-1
 and hold at 680
o
C for 2h. It can achieve the largest density of 
3.75gcm
-3
 near theoretical density and contain the least carbon content of just 38ppm. 
The meandering pattern of Pt thin film was deposited for 137 s successfully on G6 
multilayer using sputter-coating with a dimension of line width of 300Î¼m and 
thickness of 100 nm.
