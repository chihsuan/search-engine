In the evaluations of species, the genome rearrangement is the basis element. To understand 
the distance between two species, the genome rearrangement distance is a critical tool. Hence, 
computing the genome rearrangement distance is a important problem in computational 
molecular biology. Usually, we use a signed permutation to represent a special genome order. 
Computing the distances between two signed permutations has given rise to computational 
molecular biology with regard to genome rearrangement in the recent decade. There are four 
types of genome rearrangement method, reversal, translocation, fission, and fusion. Given two 
genomes represented as signed permutations of the same elements, the problems consist in 
finding the minimum number of reversals, translocations, fissions, or fusions that transforms one 
genome into the other. In the past research, we propose a graph, Signed Reversal Graph, to 
represent a kind of genome rearrangement problem, sorting by reversal problem. In this project 
we continue studied the Signed Reversal Graph and discussed more properties about the graph 
such as Fault tolerant rearrangement distance. 
Keyword. Computational biology, Graph theory, Genome rearrangement, Fault diameter.
