Abstract (Keywords: atom transfer radical polymerizationsurface-modifiedself-assembled
polymer brush) 
The polymethylmethacrylate (PMMA) polymer brush  was grafted on the silicon surface by 
using atom transfer radical polymerization (ATRP). The 4-Chloromethylphenyltrichlorosilane 
was formed the self-assembled  monolayer on the silicon surface for the initiator of polymerization 
through the hydroxyl group on the silicon wafer surface. The methylmethacrylate was induced by 
the initiator on the silicon surface to form the PMMA brush. In this work, the hydroxyl group on the 
silicon surface increases through immersion in sulfuric acid for reaction with 4-Chloromethyl
phenyltrichlorosilane as observation in decrease of contact angle for water. (ESCA) was used to 
investigate the component on the silicon surface. The PMMA brush was observed by (SEM) and 
(AFM) with feature size of 50 nm. The observation suggests that the PMMA brush aggregated in 
the toluene because the interaction of hydrogen bonding between PMMA brushes is stronger than 
the interaction of hydrogen bonding between PMMA brush and toluene. The further work is 
focused on the patterning of PMMA brush on the silicon surface for application in bioelectronics 
field.
