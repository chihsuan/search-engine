2.3 
As an important factor dominates the users' behaviors within the community, information diffusion has 
been widely studied in many aspects. Several previous works utilized dynamic process to model the diffusion 
process, focusing on analyzing the diffusion status at each time period, and users in the model react in the next 
period according to certain rules. Jackson and Yariv constructed a dynamic model in [9] describing a behavior 
adaptation process among peers within the social network. Users' utility of adapting a new behavior are 
proportional to the ratio of population adapting the same behavior of their neighbors. Their model explained 
the tipping point and the behavior adaptation rate of the diffusion system. Moreover, they discussed about the 
stability and adaptation rate under different network structures in [3]. Galeotti and Goyal discussed about a 
diffusion system consisting of an information source provider exerting effort to influence social network users 
playing as information receivers in [8] [4]. Source provider decides how much effort it should contribute 
according to its belief of the degree distribution of receivers in order to obtain more influencers under lower 
costs. Lopez-Pintado also discussed about the product spreading among social network users [5]. The 
probability of obtaining the product is also related to the diffusion rate and the interaction between 
neighboring nodes. Mean field approach is utilized to analyze the threshold for spreading rate which 
determines the life time of spreading process. The authors found that the threshold is lower when the variance 
of degree distribution is higher. As a result, the scale-free network spreads product more efficiently. Gruhl  et

In this paper, we resolve the critical issue on the network synchronization among femtocells. We 
proposed network synchronization algorithm that enables all femtocells to synchronize with and without the 
present of the Macrocell. The benefit of our algorithm is two folded: on the one hand, it has very limited 
computational complexity. Secondly, it imposes no impacts on the system architecture of the existing

Based on our previous work, an improved voter algorithm is proposed to handle the time synchronization 
in heterogeneous networks. This algorithm reduces the error probability by 90% and the convergence time by 
80% compared to the original voter algorithm. We propose a multi-hop sampling mechanism, which improves 
the convergence time of our algorithm in partially connectible networks. We show that our algorithm can be 
practicably applied to realistic network applications at the end of this paper. 
To combat the problem of unavailability of network structure in limited period when doing information 
fusion on social network, we propose a minimax robust technique for establishing reliable decision rule. 
Furthermore, with the help of percolation theory, we successfully reduce the complexity on constructing 
robust statistics. By simulations we show that our robust technique does fine performance on information 
fusion involving social network structure. 
In this paper, we propose a novel market model to solve the problem of providing satisfactory incentives 
to all SPs and all LUs among multiple WNs with different SPs in a cooperative spectrum sharing scheme,

(M2M) communications. Using a roaming rate as the incentive for each SP, we propose an adaptive iterative 
algorithm to achieve the equilibrium based on local information for decentralized implementation. With the 
equilibrium, we are able to decide the optimal roaming rate such that that the overall social welfare of all SPs 
and all LUs is maximizes.
