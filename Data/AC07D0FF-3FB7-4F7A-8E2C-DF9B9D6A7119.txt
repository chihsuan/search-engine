è‹±æ–‡æ‘˜è¦ (Abstract) 
The cyclic-prefix (CP) CDMA (CP-CDMA) is a novel type of 
single-carrier DS-CDMA that is intended for high-bandwidth, 
high delay-spread channel such as those envisioned for system 
evolving from the current 3G CDMA standard. By inserting 
sufficient redundant symbols, i.e., cyclic prefix (CP) or zero 
padding (ZP), between consecutive block symbols for 
transmissions, the inter-block interference (IBI) and the ISI 
can be suppressed, effectively. For convenience, we referred 
the CDMA system with the precoded as the precoded-CDMA 
system. In this second-year project, we present new transceiver 
framework for the DS-CDMA systems that use the 
multiple-input multiple-output (MIMO) antennas, with 
space-time block code (ST-BC). In the transmitter, new hybrid 
(prefix/postfix) zero-padding sequences combining with the 
desired user signature is exploited to form the hybrid 
augmented user signature sequences, with respect to different 
transmit-antennas. While in the receiver under the GSC 
framework a new Capon-like semi-blind MIMO-CDMA 
filterbank detector based on the linearly constrained constant 
modulus (LCCM) criterion, is derived. Also, the adaptive 
GSC-RLS algorithm is developed for adaptively implementing 
the blind LCCM MIMO-CDMA filterbank of the receiver. 
With the specific design of the hybrid augmented user 
signature sequences, we are able to resolve phase ambiguity 
problem of blind channel estimation. Computer simulations 
verify the merits of the proposed new transceiver framework; 
it can be employed to achieve better performance, and is more 
robust against the discrepancy due to Capon channel 
estimation. 
Keywords: Precoded MIMO-CDMA, LCCM criterion, GSC, 
blind channel estimation, phase ambiguity problem.

IV. SIMULATION RESULTS 
In this section, we would like to demonstrate the advantage 
of the proposed equalizing scheme, which is a cascade of the 
OB projection followed by the frequency-domain DFE. To do 
so, we consider a ST-BC MIMO OFDM system with two 
transmit antenna, M=3 receiver antennas, N=32 subcarriers, 
and the length of CP is set to be 3 for the CP-based 
transmission. The source symbols are draw from QPSK 
constellation. The background channel characteristic is tap-
delay-line (or FIR) channel model of order L, where each tap 
is independently generated according to jakes model with 
mobile speed 100 km/hr and carrier frequency is 5.18GH. 
Unless otherwise mentioned, we set channel order L=3.  Here, 
the bit error rate (BER) curves with the CP-based MIMO-
OFDM (or in short OFDM with CP) are chosen as the 
benchmark for comparison. From Fig. 3, the MMSE- and ZF- 
(zero-forcing) OFDM systems, without CP, perform worse 
than the proposed OB-FD-DFE (zero-forcing) scheme and the 
CP-based OFDM approach. Next, it is of interest to 
investigate the effect of increasing the delay-spread length. 
From Fig. 4, for L=5 and with other parameters are the same 
as in Fig. 3, we observed that the proposed scheme performed 
very close to the CP-based OFDM system. Thus, we 
concluded that the proposed scheme, without CP, could be 
used to eliminate the ISI and ICI, as that using the CP-based 
OFDM system, but has more efficiency of spectral utilization 
with the cost of extra signal processing computational loading 
done in the receiver.  
V.  CONCLUSIONS 
In this paper, we proposed a novel frequency-domain DFE 
equalizer, associated with the oblique projector, for the CP-
free space-time coding MIMO-OFDM system, with two 
transmit-antennas and M (M =3) receive-antennas. Since the 
number of subcarrier N is typically much larger than the 
channel order, L, we proved that the choice of 3M   the 
degrees of freedom was enough to satisfy the condition, 
2 2MN N L  , for combating the loss due to ICI. As 
verified in the simulation results, the performance of CP-free 
system is compatible with that using the CP systems.
