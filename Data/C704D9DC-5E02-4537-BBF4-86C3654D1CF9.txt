Applying HTGA to Determine the Optimal Coefficient of 
DOE in the Chromatic Aberration of Gaussian Lens  
 
aCheng-Mu Tsai, bYi-Chin Fang, and bHan-Ching Lin  
aDept. of Computer and Communication, Kun Shan University  
bDept. of Mechanical and Automation Engineering, National Kaohsiung First University of Science and Technology College of Engineering 
ae-mail: jmutsai@gmail.com  
 
Abstract: The diffractive optical element (DOE) with a new method Hybrid Taguchi Genetic 
Algorism (HTGA) design is proposed to eliminate chromatic aberration in zoom optics. According 
to diffractive theory, the DOE with negative Abbe number can be realized to mount on lens 
surface so as to improve the chromatic aberration. The steady Taguchi method incorporated with 
the genetic algorithm (GA), called hybrid Taguchi-genetic algorithm (HTGA), is applied layout 
350x zoom lens in the proposal. The results show the chromatic aberration is improved through 
using HTGA with DOE element.  
 
1. Introduction  
The optical system now faces many challenges because of pushing the lens limits into relatively high zoom ratio, 
angle of field of view requirements, system total length of compression limit, and so forth. Fortunately, the task of 
optimization in optical systems now works efficiently, partly because since 1960 such technologies have been 
employed as advances in computing, aspherical surfaces, antireflection coatings and new developments in optical 
glass. However, the traditional optimal method, Least Damping Square (LDS), has been less than the capability to 
design the modern zoom lens system so that optical design with better optimal method becomes urgent to be 
developed [1-2].  
There are two different ways that could be applied in lens design to improve the chromatic aberration. The 
common method is to choice suitable glass materials combination so that the chromatic aberration could be canceled 
out through piece by piece or cluster by cluster lens. Alternately, the other applies diffractive optical element (DOE) 
with negative Abbe number characteristic to minimum chromatic aberration. Since the DOE can be fabricated 
through basic optical materials like plastic lens, it does not occupy space nor does it add weight on the system. This 
is a great advantage for developing a thinner, lighter optical system.  
The LDS method is most frequently used for optical system optimization, including the DOEâ€™s coefficient and 
glass selections. However, it is easy to fall into local solutions that limit the optimization of results [3]. Many 
scholars were focusing on the simultaneous optimization of different types of imaging aberrations [4-5], but none 
achieved favorable results; therefore, some began to adopt the genetic algorithm (GA) [1-2]. The invention of hybrid 
Taguchi-genetic algorithm (HTGA) was a nice addition to the tools used to render solutions [6-7]. This method, 
which combines the powerful GA and the steady Taguchi method, has been used globally. In this paper, we use 
HTGA to target the DOEâ€™s coefficient and optical glass combination in place of the more familiar damped least 
square method to maximize the DOEâ€™s effects on eliminating chromatic aberration and to help optical designers to 
receive the best results within the shortest amount of time.  
In this paper, section 2 introduces HTGA, in theory and processes and other related parameter setups. In section 
3, a 350X high zoom ratio lens will be designed. HTGA will be adopted to optimize glass and DOE. The 
effectiveness of eliminating chromatic aberration will be discussed. Section 4 makes concluding remarks. 
2. Elimination of Chromatic Aberration based on HTGA   
The central tenet in deploying GA rests upon the concepts of natural selection and the survival of the fittest [4]. The 
fundamentals of the use of GA simulate the competition among organisms, in which those that are most fit have the 
right to increase their posterity. Therefore, the GA process can be begun by randomly selecting an initial population 
and then allowing reproduction, crossover, and mutation to proceed, according to the fitness function [5]. The fitness 
function in the proposal is defined as:  
FitValue = w1AX + w2LAT + w3EFL   (1)

4. Conclusion  
A 350x zoom lens is used to improve its chromatic aberration by applying HTGA with DOE. Four DOEs are 
investigated the efficiency about the chromatic aberration suppression. Optimal results suggest that DOE is added to 
the last element (S35). Although the AXs are not obviously improved for zooms 1 and 2, the AXs are acceptable for 
zooms 1 and 2. However, the AX of initial layout is not allowed for zoom 3. After the HTGA optimization, 98% 
increase of AX and there is significantly improvement for AX; 98% from 2.6728 to 0.05294197 for Zoom 3 position. 
According to simulation data for LAT, 32% improvement for Zoom1 position, 38% for Zoom 2 position, and 63% 
for Zoom 3 position. Conclusively, HTGA has great contribution to seeking solutions and eliminating chromatic 
aberration cooperated with DOE.
