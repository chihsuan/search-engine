The steady propagation of a premixed flame in an isothermal tube subject to the Poiseuille 
flow is numerically investigated in this study. The complete Navier-Stokes equations are used in 
the mathematical formulation and the flame chemistry is modeled by an one-step overall reaction. 
The numerical results show that the flame propagating steadily in tubes opposed to the Poiseuille 
flow can take two distinct shapes: symmetric mushroom shape and asymmetrical slant shape. For 
example, the symmetric mushroom-shaped flame can be found in a small channel, but in a larger 
channel, only asymmetrical slant shape flame exists. The flame structure of the present result for 
the larger channel is totally different to that of using axisymmetric assumption. The critical wall 
velocity gradient for flame flashback is depicted in this study, and the trend concurs with the 
experimental results. The behaviors of flame symmetry are examined as a function of the 
intensity of incoming velocity profile, tube diameter (or channel width) and the gravity along the 
tube. It is found that the above parameters not only modify the flame burning speed, they also 
affect the flame shape and its symmetrical behavior. The investigation of stability problem for 
various flames will be conducted in near future. The numerical results show that the stationary 
propagating flame in a square tube with isothermal wall can take three distinct shapes: tulip shape!
mushroom shape and hybrid shape. 
Keywords: flame, asymmetry
